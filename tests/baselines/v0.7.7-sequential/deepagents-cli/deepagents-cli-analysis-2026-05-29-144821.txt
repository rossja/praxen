BEHAVIOR SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The CLI's central guarantee — that the bundle faithfully and only reflects the
developer's reviewed project — holds at the bundler level: `bundle()` in
`deepagents_cli/deploy/bundler.py` reads exactly the declared sources and the
anonymous-deploy confirmation prompt in `execute_deploy_command` fires before
any push when `[frontend].enabled` and `[auth].provider = "anonymous"`. But
several declared protections are narrower than their remit wording: the
anonymous-auth confirmation only fires when a `[frontend]` is configured (a
frontend-less anonymous deploy ships without the prompt), the MCP transport
validator at `deploy/config.py:_validate_mcp_for_deploy` rejects stdio but
does not enforce `https://` for http/sse URLs, remote MCP servers are carried
into the bundle with no integrity check or version pin (a remit-stated MUST),
and the deploy tooling installs no log handlers so `logger.info` events in
`bundler.py` / `templates.py` are invisible to the operator by default. The
`THREAT_MODEL.md` is preserved at `libs/cli/THREAT_MODEL.md` but its own
disclaimer flags it as describing the pre-0.1.0 interactive-REPL surface — a
SHOULD in the remit's Escalation section that is now out of step with what the
package actually ships.
